Thailand Must Go Destinations

Bangkok

Wat Arun

This iconic temple, with its intricate porcelain-clad spires, offers stunning views of the Chao Phraya River, especially at sunset. I am drawn to its unique architectural style and historical significance, providing a glimpse into Thailand's rich cultural heritage.

How to get there:
Take the Chao Phraya River Express boat to Tha Tien pier, then a short ferry crossing to Wat Arun.

Temple of the Emerald Buddha

A must-see for first-time visitors, the Grand Palace showcases the opulence of Thai royalty and houses the revered Emerald Buddha. The complex's elaborate architecture and historical significance offer a deep dive into Thai history and art.

How to get there:
Easily accessible by taxi or tuk-tuk.

Chatuchak Weekend Market

One of the world's largest weekend markets, Chatuchak offers a vast array of goods, from clothing and handicrafts to food and pets. It's a shopping paradise and a cultural experience, attracting both locals and tourists seeking unique finds.

How to get there:
Take the BTS Skytrain to Mo Chit station or the MRT subway to Chatuchak Park station.

Chiang Mai

Doi Suthep

Perched on a mountain overlooking Chiang Mai, Doi Suthep is a sacred temple with stunning views and a tranquil atmosphere. I am drawn to its golden chedi, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance.

How to get there:
Take a taxi from Chiang Mai city.

Elephant Sanctuaries

Ethical elephant sanctuaries offer visitors the chance to interact with these gentle giants in a responsible way.

How to get there:
Many sanctuaries offer transportation from Chiang Mai city.

Wat Chedi Luang

Wat Chedi Luang is a significant historical and architectural landmark in Chiang Mai's Old City. Its most striking feature is the large, partially ruined chedi, which once housed the Emerald Buddha.

How to get there:
A Songthaews are a common and affordable mode of transportation in Chiang Mai.
